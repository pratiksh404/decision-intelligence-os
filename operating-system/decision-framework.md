# Decision Framework

## Purpose

Create decisions that are evidence-based, probability-aware, and reviewable.

## Required Flow

1. Question
2. Research
3. Evidence Collection
4. Analysis
5. Counterarguments
6. Probability Assessment
7. Decision
8. Execution Plan
9. Review Schedule

## Decision Quality Standard

A decision is ready when:

- The decision question is explicit.
- Facts and assumptions are separated.
- Alternatives are named.
- Evidence and counter-evidence are documented.
- Risks are visible.
- Probabilities are assigned.
- The final decision is recorded.
- Next actions have owners and dates.
- A review date exists.

## Decision Types

- Proceed
- Monitor
- Reject
- Delay
- Escalate Research
- Run Experiment

## Review Questions

- What did we believe at decision time?
- Which assumptions were most important?
- What evidence would have changed the decision?
- What happened after execution?
- Was the forecast calibrated?
- What should be updated in the system?
